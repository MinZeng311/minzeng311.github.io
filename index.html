Presentations

  1. Mandarin Trilinguals' Perception of L3 Japanese Stops.
    Min Zeng
    L3 Workshop 2024 (Tokyo) .
    Abstract Poster

    2. This study explored the acquisition of Japanese word-initial voiceless stops by Mandarin trilinguals with advanced proficiency in their third language Japanese and the relationship between their full-time equivalent (FTE) years of Japanese input and their perception of these stops. Thirty-one Mandarin trilinguals and 34 Japanese bilinguals completed a perception experiment by listening to Japanese stop continua with synthetic stimuli varying in voice onset time (VOT) values. The results showed that the Mandarin trilinguals perceived Japanese stops with category boundaries occurring at longer VOT values than the Japanese bilinguals. The trilinguals' perception skills revealed phonetic interference from both their native Mandarin and second language English. The correlation analysis between the trilinguals' FTE and their mean category boundaries indicated that longer FTE for trilinguals may not directly translate into target-like performance.
